## Bill of Rights (first 10)

1. Protects Freedom of speech, religion, the press, assembly, and petition. It prohibits Congress from restricting these rights, but does not apply to private individuals or companies. \
2. Protects the Right to bear Arms. Originally intented for militia, it has been passed to the individual for many years. \
3. Prevents quartering of soldiers in houses. Written directly in response to actions of the Enlish Crown \
4. Prevents unreasonable search and seizure, specifically requiring a warrant prior to searching. \ 
5. Requires a jury for criminal trials, prevents double-jeopardy (try for the same crime twice), and prevents self-incrimination (Miranda laws). \
6. Protects the right to a "speedy trial" (vague) and requires the perpetrator to be informed of the crimes brought against him. \
7. Protects trial by jury in civil cases
8. Prevents excessive bail, fines, or "cruel and unusual" punishments. \
9. Protects the "non=enumerated" rights. Ex. The right to Privacy is not specified, but is a basic right. \
10. Protects the power of the people and states by limiting the power of the federal government. \
